My International Experience — Gabon → France

Rebecca Ndouga • ISEN Yncréa Ouest, Brest

Why France?

I chose France mainly because of the language — French is widely spoken in Gabon — and because I have family here. My uncle lives in Brittany, which made the move less intimidating.

Why Brest?

I wanted to stay close to my uncle in Brittany. I applied to schools in the region and was accepted at ISEN Brest, a recognized engineering school.

What surprised me
Weather
Big seasonal changes — winter is much colder than what I'm used to in Gabon.
Food
Different products and flavors; it took time to adapt.
People
More reserved social behavior compared to Gabon — initially surprising.
Distance
Being far from family is emotionally difficult, even with relatives and new friends around.
Highlights & Travels

I love the landscapes and architecture here. Brest offers beaches and many activities. I travelled to several French cities — notably Bordeaux, where I completed my M1 internship. The experience was very positive: friendly people and a beautiful city.

Personal takeaways

This experience helped me grow, discover a new culture, and gain independence. It also encouraged me to explore more countries in the future.
